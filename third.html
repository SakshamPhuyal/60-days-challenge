Description

Water is a transparent, tasteless, odorless, and nearly colorless chemical substance that is essential for all known forms of life.

It covers about 71% of Earth's surface, primarily in seas, oceans, and other large water bodies.

Chemical Properties

Water has a neutral pH of 7 under standard conditions.

It can act as both an acid and a base (amphoteric).

H2O + H+ → H3O+ H2O + OH− → HO− + H2O
Structure of water

Water is a molecule composed of two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom (H2O).

Its structure is:

  • Bent/angular shape with a bond angle of about 104.5°
  • Polar molecule due to unequal sharing of electrons (oxygen is more electronegative than hydrogen)
  • Hydrogen bonds form between water molecules, leading to unique properties like high boiling point, surface tension, and solvent ability
Importance

Water is essential for all forms of life.

It plays a vital role in maintaining bodily functions, supporting agriculture, enabling industry, and sustaining ecosystems. Without clean and accessible water, survival and development would not be possible.

It is used as a solvent, coolant, and raw material in various industries.

Conservation Measures

The conservation measures are:

  • Rainwater Harvesting
  • Reuse and Recycle Water
  • Turn Off Taps When Not in Use and fix leaks
  • Water-Efficient Landscaping
  • Educate and Raise Awareness
  • Drip Irrigation
